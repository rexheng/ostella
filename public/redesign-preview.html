The opportunity

A market the NHS has been told to fix.

The economics of preventative women's health are unusually clean. The cohort is large, the window is narrow, and the cost of missing it is well-documented.

Why the window matters

A five-year window the NHS can't currently see.

Perimenopause — roughly ages 42 to 55 — is when women's long-run health trajectories are set. The interventions that change them are cheap, behavioural, and well-understood. The problem is timing: by the time the symptoms force a conversation, the window has usually closed.

Ostella is calibrated for exactly this cohort. It reads the clinical history a GP already has, scores it against a transparent model built on published evidence, and surfaces the minority of women who need a preventative conversation now.

How it works

Three steps from screening to action.

01

Score the cohort

Every woman on a GP's list is scored against a transparent linear model built on published clinical evidence.

02

Clinician reviews

A named GP sees the model's reasoning per patient and decides whether to initiate a preventative conversation.

03

Patient acts

Books an appointment, reads the education library, or self-refers — from a portal designed for trust, not engagement metrics.
